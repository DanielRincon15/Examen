The Formula 1 teams are a select club of teams of drivers licensed to compete in the races that make up the F1 World Cup. Most of these teams are associated with major automobile brands, although more and more they are linked to large sponsors who invest fortunes to participate in "the great circus." Each team competes in the race with two drivers, and they have as many test drivers. From there, each team has a different organization chart, although they all have a multitude of engineers, who carry out a job almost as important as that of the drivers. In F1 a day we show you the most relevant teams, being able to consult their best historical results in Formula 1.


MOST IMPORTANT TEAMS

REDBULL

Red Bull Racing is an Austrian Formula 1 team based in Milton Keynes (England) owned by the energy drink company Red Bull. The company acquired the Jaguar Racing team for nearly $ 110 million when the team's previous owner, Ford Motor Company, announced its withdrawal from motorsport's top flight. The Red Bull company owns a Formula 1 subsidiary team, Scuderia AlphaTauri (known until 2019 as Scuderia Toro Rosso), based in Faenza (Italy), from which the young drivers subsequently make the jump to the first team. He has competed since 2005 and in his first two seasons he raced under a British license.


MERCEDES-BENZ

Mercedes-Benz is one of the most successful automakers in Formula 1, considered one of the four best-performing teams, along with Williams, Ferrari and McLaren. It was initially involved as a constructor in Formula 1 in 1954 and 1955, and from 2010 to the actuality. The German team won the last seven Constructors' Championships in a row (from 2014 to 2020). It is the third team with the most Drivers' Championships with nine (1954, 1955, 2014, 2015, 2016, 2017, 2018, 2019 and 2020), as well as the third team in history with the most victories (121) and poles (131) in all the previous items, only behind Ferrari and McLaren.


MCLAREN

McLaren Racing Limited, generally known as McLaren or, also, McLaren F1 Team, is a British motorsports team based in Woking (Surrey, England), founded in 1963 by Bruce McLaren. It is considered one of the four great Formula 1 teams, along with Williams, Ferrari and Mercedes. Over the years it has won 8 Constructors 'Championships (third in history), 12 Drivers' Championships (second), 183 victories. (second), 155 poles (second) and 489 podiums (second).


FERRARI

Scuderia Ferrari is the sports division (Gestione Sportiva) of Ferrari cars in charge of Formula 1 competitions. Recently, Ferrari Corse Clienti has been in charge of almost all the other sports activities of the company and the care of private teams. Ferrari's first participation in Formula 1 was in the 1950 Monaco Grand Prix, with the Type 125 F1. Ferrari is considered one of the four great teams in Formula 1, along with Williams, McLaren and Mercedes.7 It is the oldest active team in the championship, and is the one with the most victories, drivers 'championships (15) and constructors' championships. WILLIAMS

Williams Racing, commonly known as Williams, is a Formula 1 team created in 1977 by Frank Williams and Patrick Head. It was founded under the name of Williams Grand Prix Engineering, having different name changes over the years. Williams is considered one of the four great Formula 1 teams, along with Ferrari, McLaren and Mercedes. The team's first race was at the 1977 Spanish Grand Prix, when the team raced on a March chassis and Patrick Nève as pilot. Williams began building his own cars the following year. Swiss Clay Regazzoni won the first race for the team at the 1979 British Grand Prix, while at the 1997 British Grand Prix, Canadian Jacques Villeneuve won the 100th race for the team. In this way, Williams became one of only three teams in Formula 1, together with Ferrari and McLaren, also English, to win 100 races.
